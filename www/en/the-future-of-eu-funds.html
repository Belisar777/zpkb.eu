The Future of European Funds

On March 27, 2025, the Pilsen Region Representation in Brussels hosted a very important event, the significance of which was underscored by the participation of Czech Minister for Regional Development Petr Kulhánek himself. The event was a presentation of the Czech Republic’s position on the future of cohesion policy for the period 2028-2035, in other words, what we are bringing to the European discussion on how European funds will be distributed in the future. Within the EU, the Czech Republic belongs to the group of so-called “net recipients,” i.e., those that receive more from the European budget than they contribute to it. This is evident to citizens from the frequent signs at larger construction sites announcing that the work is co-financed by the European Union. 

Within the group of “net recipient” countries, we are also among the “good students,” which means that the Czech Republic is experiencing economic growth, to which European financial injections undoubtedly contribute. Therefore, the future of this support is absolutely crucial for us, not only for the republic as a whole, but also for us as the Pilsen Region and our municipalities.

That is why the mutual debate with the minister was so important and, we believe, mutually beneficial, as the opinions and recommendations expressed on our part are based on years of experience with drawing on European funds. These opinions were conveyed to the minister and his entourage primarily by Martin Rybár, Deputy Governor of the Pilsen Region for European Affairs and IT.
