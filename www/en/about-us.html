About us

We promote the interests of the Pilsen Region in the European Commission, the European Parliament and the Committee of the Regions. We monitor the development of EU policies that have a significant impact on our region. We raise awareness of our region in Brussels by connecting cultures and businesses.

History of the Representation

The European Office of the Pilsen Region in Brussels began its activities on 3 January 2006 as a detached workplace of the Regional Authority of the Pilsen Region. Its first representative was Karel Lizerot.

To facilitate the start of its operations, colleagues from the East Midlands European Office (East Midlands also includes the Northamptonshire region, which is a partner region of the Plzeň Region), based at Avenue d’Auderghem 22–28, 1040 Brussels, provided office facilities.


The office was based there until June 2007, when it moved to its own rented premises in the so-called Czech House at Rue du Trône 60, B-1050 Brussels. The Czech House in Brussels was a project related to the first-ever Czech Presidency of the Council of the EU, which took place in the first half of 2009. For this purpose, the Permanent Representation of the Czech Republic to the EU needed to increase the number of diplomats and secure appropriate premises. These were found in a building directly adjacent to the Permanent Representation at Rue Caroly 15, where the Ministry of Foreign Affairs of the Czech Republic, as the founder of the Permanent Representation, rented the entire office building. However, the Permanent Representation required only part of the building, so the remaining premises were offered to other Czech offices in Brussels. As a result, the offices of Czech Airlines, Czech Railways, CzechTrade/CzechInvest, CzechTourism, the Czech Centre, the Czech Business Representation to the EU (CEBRE), the Embassy of the Czech Republic in Belgium, and a “regional floor” for Czech regions moved under one roof.

Not all regions made use of this opportunity. The following remained outside:

  • The Capital City of Prague, as it has had its own premises in Brussels at Avenue Palmerston 16 since June 2002;

  • The Central Bohemian Region, which at that time rented its own building at Avenue d’Auderghem 45, Brussels-Etterbeek;

  • The South Bohemian Region and the Liberec Region, which sublet offices from the Central Bohemian Region;

  • The Zlin and Olomouc Regions, which were represented by a lobbying agency.

The Czech House therefore hosted the offices of the South Moravian Region, Karlovy Vary Region, Hradec Kralove Region, Moravian-Silesian Region, Pardubice Region, Pilsen Region, Usti Region and the Vysocina Region.

At that time, the representative of the Pilsen Region in Brussels was Mgr. Zbyněk Prokop, who took over the agenda from Karel Lizerot on 1 January 2007.

Zbyněk Prokop

The Czech House project in Brussels, which aimed to provide shared premises for all Czech offices in Brussels under favourable conditions, was a very good idea—unfortunately too good to survive. The Czech Republic did not complete the purchase of the rented building, and the Czech House project in Brussels ended in 2011.

Where to Find Us and How to Reach Us

The Office of the Pilsen Region is located at Place de Jamblinne de Meux 31, Brussels – Schaerbeek, B-1030. The building is close to the headquarters of the European Commission and the Council of the EU at Rond-Point Schuman, and not far from the European Parliament at Rue Wiertz and the Committee of the Regions at Rue Belliard.

From the airport, the easiest way is by express bus no. 12 (Meiser or Schuman stop) or no. 21 (stop almost in front of the building – Jamblinne de Meux).

By metro, the nearest station is Schuman on lines 1 and 5, followed by approximately a 10-minute walk or a short bus ride (bus no. 21) for two stops to Jamblinne de Meux.
