Students in Brussels

From March 3 to 5, 2025, another trip to Brussels was organized by the Student Parliament of the Pilsen Region under the auspices of the Radovánek Leisure Center.

The aim of the excursion was to give students an insight into the functioning of European institutions. To this end, they visited the European Parliament’s visitor center, the Parlamentarium, and the headquarters of the European Committee of the Regions. The group also visited the headquarters of the Pilsen Region Representation in Brussels, where two Czech MEPs, – Tomáš Zdechovský (European People’s Party/KDU-ČSL) and Ondřej Dostál (independent/Stačilo!) – together with former governor and member of the Committee of the Regions Rudolf Špoták (Greens and Progressives/independent) and deputy governor Martin Rybár (KDU-ČSL). The questions the students asked the politicians showed that they are interested in public affairs and are not indifferent to developments in our country.

The next day, before returning home, the students visited the historic center of Brussels and the iconic model of an iron atom up-scaled  165 billion times, the Atomium.
